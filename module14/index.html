Frequently Asked Questions

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

Look for cracks, dents, or any other signs of damage that may have occurred over time, such as worn out straps or padding.

It may be safe to ride your bike after it has been in storage, but it's recommended to check the tires, brakes, chain, and gears for proper function and make necessary adjustments. Also, it's good to take it for a test ride, to ensure everything is working properly before taking it on longer trips.

When riding a bike, it's important to follow traffic laws, such as obeying traffic signals and signs, riding with the flow of traffic, and using hand signals to indicate turns. It's also important to wear a helmet, use lights and reflectors at night, and be visible to other road users.
